Discovery

Tungsten was initially discovered in 1779ce by an Irish chemist known as Peter Woulfe. Then in 1781ce, it was isolated as tungsten oxide in Sweden by Carl W. Scheele from the mineral scheelite (calcium tungstate). Sadly, he did not own a furnace powerful enough to reduce the oxide to the metal. After that in 1783ce, tungsten was finally isolated by two brothers, Fauto and Juan Jose de Elhuyar, in Spain.

List of Common Uses

Tungsten is used...

  • to strengthen other metals by smelting and mixing them together.
  • in lightbulbs as filaments.
  • for making strong and durable cutting tools.
  • for medical used X-ray tubes.
  • in computer monitors for creating the screen.

Fun Fact!

Tungsten is known to be one of the strongest things in nature, it is very durable, dense and almost impossible to melt!.
