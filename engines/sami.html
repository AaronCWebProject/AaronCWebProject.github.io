Information about Iridium

Atomic Number77
Atomic SymbolIr
Atomic Weight(average mass of the atom): 192.217
Melting Point4,435 F (2,446 C)
Boiling Point8,002.4 F (4,428 C)
Stable Isotopes2, which are iridium-191 (37.3 percent) and iridium-193 (62.7 percent)

What is Iridium used for?

Iridium is a very tough and heavy-duty metal. Although very brittle when in its pure form, it can be mixed with other materials to make it more durable and to improve its performance. Because of it having a high melting point and being a good conductor of electricity, iridium is commonly used in spark plugs, where the explosions of an engine happen (it does not melt because of its high melting point). It easily beats coper 2 main reasons, because copper is a very soft material compared to iridium (when not in its pure form), and iridium does not corrode like other metals making it last much longer when used in hardware. But there is one problem. Iridium is extremely rare to find, that is why it is so expensive. So how much does it cost?? 1 gram in USD (pure form) is $1660.

When was Iridium Found?

Iridium was first discovered in 1803 by the English chemist Smithson Tennant. iridium is naturally mixed with other metals when found, it is very rare to find pure iridium. Metals mixed (naturally) with this unique material can be found in places such as, Alaska, U.S., as well as in Myanmar (Burma), Brazil, Russia, and Australia. In the late 20th century South Africa was the world's major producer of iridium.
